Mash Daal with Tadka

Description

The black gram or urad bean (Vigna mungo) is a bean grown in South Asia. Like its relative the mung bean, it has been reclassified from the Phaseolus to the Vigna genus. The product sold as black gram is usually the whole urad bean, whereas the split bean (the interior being white) is called white lentil. It should not be confused with the much smaller true black lentil (Lens culinaris).

Origin

Black gram originated in South Asia, where it has been in cultivation from ancient times and is one of the most highly prized pulses of India. It is very widely used in Indian cuisine. In India the black gram is one of the important pulses grown in both Kharif and Rabi seasons. This crop is extensively grown in the southern part of India and the northern part of Bangladesh and Nepal. In Bangladesh and Nepal it is known as mash daal. It is a popular daal (legume) side dish in South Asia that goes with curry and rice as a platter. Black gram has also been introduced to other tropical areas such as the Caribbean, Fiji, Mauritius, Myanmar and Africa mainly by Indian immigrants during the Indian indenture system.

Ingredients

Base Dish

  • Ghee ¼ Cup
  • Laung (Cloves) 5-6
  • Sabut kali mirch (Black peppercorns) 6-8
  • Adrak (Ginger) julienne 1 inch piece
  • Pyaz (Onion) chopped 1 medium
  • Lehsan (Garlic) crushed 1 tbs
  • Hari mirch (Green chilies) 2-3
  • Tamatar (Tomatoes) grinded 2 medium
  • Hing (Asafeotida) powder ¼ tsp
  • Lal mirch powder (Red chili powder) 1 tsp or to taste
  • Haldee powder (Turmeric powder) ¼ tsp
  • Namak (Salt) 1 tsp or to taste
  • Mash daal (White lentil gram) 250 gms (soaked & boiled with salt)
  • Pani (Water) 1 Cup or as required
  • Hara dhania (Fresh coriander) chopped 1-2 tbs
  • Garam masala powder ½ tsp

Tadka: (Optional)

  • Cooking oil 2-3 tbs
  • Sabut lal mirch (Whole red chilies) 5-6
  • Zeera (Cumin seeds) ½ tsp
  • Lal mirch powder (Red chili powder) ¼ tsp
  • Adrak (Ginger) julienne
  • Hara dhania (Fresh coriander) chopped

Steps

  1. In pot, add ghee and let it melt, add cloves, black peppercorns and mix.
  2. Add ginger and mix well.
  3. Add onion and fry until light brown.
  4. Add garlic and mix well.
  5. Add green chilies and fry for a minute.
  6. Add grinded tomatoes and mix well.
  7. Add asafeotida powder, red chili powder, turmeric powder and salt, mix well and cook for 2-3 minutes.
  8. Ad boiled white lentil gram and mix well.
  9. Add water and mix well.
  10. Add fresh coriander and garam masala powder, cover and let it simmer for 2 minutes.Its ready to serve!

Tadka: (Optional)

  1. In small frying pan, add cooking oil, whole red chilies, cumin seeds and fry for 1 minute.
  2. Turn off the flame, add red chili powder and mix.
  3. Add tadka on cooked white lentil gram and garnish with ginger, fresh coriander & serve.
