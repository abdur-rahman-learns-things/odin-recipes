Description

Hyderabadi biryani (also known as Hyderabadi dum biryani) is a style of biryani originating from Hyderabad, India made with basmati rice and meat (mostly goat meat). Originating in the kitchens of the Nizam of Hyderabad, it combines elements of Hyderabadi and Mughlai cuisines. Hyderabad biryani is a key dish in Hyderabadi cuisine and it is so famous that the dish is considered synonymous with the city of Hyderabad.

Origin

The exact origin of the dish is uncertain. Despite legends attributing it to the Nizam's chef, the biryani is of South Indian origin, derived from pilaf varieties brought to South Asia by Arab traders. Pulao may have been an army dish in medieval India. Armies would prepare a one-pot dish of rice with whichever meat was available. The distinction between "pulao" and "biryani" is arbitrary. Hyderabadi biryani developed engrossing Deccani or Telangana flavors into it, as stated by the Himayat Ali Mirza, the great-grandson of Mir Osman Ali Khan. Himayat said that this evolution had taken place in the Asaf Jah's Kitchen.

Ingredients

  • 1 Royal® Hyderabadi Biryani kit (includes authentic basmati rice, biryani paste and whole spices)
  • 1lb chicken, cut into pieces (bone-in or boneless)
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 green chilies, slit lengthwise
  • 2 cups mixed vegetables (such as carrots, peas, potatoes, bell peppers, cauliflower), diced
  • 2 tablespoons ghee or oil
  • 1/4 cup chopped mint leaves
  • 1/4 cup chopped coriander leaves
  • Salt to taste
  • Water as per the package instructions

Steps

  1. Wash and soak the basmati rice from the Royal® Hyderabadi Biryani Kit for 20-30 minutes (optional).
  2. Add 4 cups of water, 1 tbsp ghee or oil and whole spices to saucepan and bring to boil.
  3. Add soaked rice to boiling water and boil uncovered for 4-5 minutes. Drain and set aside.
  4. Heat ghee or oil in a sauté pan over medium heat. Add the thinly sliced onions and sauté until golden brown. Now add the diced mixed vegetables and sauté for a few minutes until they are slightly tender.
  5. Add chicken to the pan and sauté for a few minutes until it is slightly cooked.
  6. Add the biryani paste and sauté for 2-3 minutes.
  7. Add 1/3 cup of water and sauté until chicken is fully cooked.
  8. Set aside half the gravy mixture for layering of the final dish.
  9. In the same sauté pan, layer half cooked rice on top of gravy. On top of it, place chicken. Continue layering with the rest of gravy, then rest of rice, then rest of chicken. Cover with lid and slow cook in low heat for 5 minutes.
  10. Serve the Chicken Biryani hot, garnished with additional chopped mint leaves and coriander leaves if desired.
  11. Enjoy your aromatic and flavorful Chicken Biryani prepared using the Royal® Hyderabadi Biryani kit!
